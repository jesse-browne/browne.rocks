How to get there

The Hotel Oregon in McMinnville is an awesome place to stay.

There's always a charming AirBnB.

Camping at Rainflower Gardens is also an option.

What to do during your visit

Whether your venturing to Oregon for a few nights or a few weeks, there is plenty in store to keep you busy. City vistas, stunning forests, river drives, and wine tours are sure to keep you going day in and day out.

Portland is a great home base to explore much of the lush northwest portion of the state. From here there are several day drives worth mentioning.

The Columbia River Gorge

The Mt. Hood Scenic byway

We would even suggest taking a couple days and doing the whole loop from Mt. Hood through the Gorge. You can find excellent accommodation at Timberline Lodge on Mt. Hood, or at the Hood River Hotel in Hood River.

The wedding will be on acreage outside of McMinnville about an hour and a half southwest of Portland. On the drive out to McMinnville you will pass winery after winery. Definitely check some out along the way and allow for additional time for deep chats with viticulture experts and strolls through the grape vineyards.

Local accommodation can be found in McMinnville at the McMenamins Hotel Oregon located on Third Street. Be sure to check out the rooftop bar at the Hotel Oregon, where you can sip (more) pinot noir and enjoy a nice view of McMinnville and the greater Yamhill Valley. Third Street is jam packed with cute boutiques, delicious restaurants, and fine wine bars.

Although McMinnville is small, it has lots of charm, and in just 45 short minutes you can find yourself at the Oregon coast and the Pacific Ocean. There are other lodging options as well in the form of motels, B&B, and camping at Rainflower Gardens where the wedding is if you are into that.

We invite you to go on an adventure with us, and to make your time in Oregon as thrilling or as relaxing as you wish. We are really excited to share this amazing opportunity with you all and do let us know if you want more suggestions to add to your itinerary. The Great Pacific Northwest holds many treasures beyond what's outlined here!
